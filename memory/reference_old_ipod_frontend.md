**"old 2000's ipod frontend"** refers to the aesthetic shown in the saved screenshot: two classic iPod devices (iPod Photo/classic era, mid-2000s) displaying their iconic UI.

Key visual characteristics:
- **Hardware**: Glossy white/silver polycarbonate body, circular click wheel with center button, minimalist industrial design
- **Now Playing screen**: Small album art thumbnail (left), track title + artist + album in clean sans-serif, progress bar in blue/light blue, track count (e.g. "6 of 15"), elapsed time in small text
- **Menu screen**: Dark blue highlight bar on selected item, white list items, right-facing chevron arrows for navigation, "iPod" branding at top in small caps
- **Typography**: Clean, small, system-level sans-serif — tight leading, no decoration
- **Color palette**: White device, grey/silver accents, blue selection highlight, black text on white background for menus
- **Overall feel**: Extremely minimal, utilitarian, iconic — every pixel functional, no ornamentation

When the user says "old 2000's ipod frontend" they want UI to evoke this exact era — click-wheel navigation metaphors, list-based menus with selection highlights, now-playing layouts with small album art and track metadata.
